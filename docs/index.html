How It Works

From target to ranked candidates in seconds

Proteus automates the entire in-silico design loop — no PhD required to run it.

01

Input a Target

Provide a protein target by name, PDB ID, or upload a .pdb file. Proteus auto-detects the binding site and structure.

02

Run MCMC

Metropolis-Hastings sampling across parallel temperature chains. BLOSUM62 and ESM-2 guide biologically plausible mutations.

Each design is scored on binding affinity, folding stability, solubility, charge, and hydrophobicity simultaneously.

Top-ranked candidates sorted by binding score, with full mutation history and 3D structure viewer. Export as JSON or CSV.

Convergence Diagnostics

Gelman-Rubin R-hat measures inter-chain variance. R-hat < 1.05 indicates all 5 chains have converged to the same distribution — not just a local optimum. Reported per run.

Effective Sample Size (ESS) measures autocorrelation-adjusted sample count. ESS > 100 is required for statistical validity of the energy landscape estimate.

Energy Decomposition

The composite energy function is a weighted sum of six terms:

E = 0.50 × (1 − binding)
+ 0.25 × (1 − stability)
+ 0.15 × (1 − solubility)
+ 0.10 × (1 − BBB score)
+ 0.08 × aggregation
+ 0.02 × charge penalty

Weights are adjustable per target. Constraint flags rebalance the objective automatically.

Mutation Operators

Four proposal operators sample biologically plausible mutations:

Point substitution — BLOSUM62 weighted
Single insertion — ESM-2 context guided
Single deletion — length-penalized
Block swap — 3–6 residue segment shuffle

Methodology Note

Binding affinity values (nM) for Proteus candidates are estimated using a multi-objective energy oracle combining BLOSUM62 substitution log-odds, ESM-2 pseudo-likelihood scores, and a hydrophobicity/charge penalty term — calibrated against published IC50 values for known binders on the same targets. Published drug values (Erlotinib, Pembrolizumab, Sotorasib) are sourced from peer-reviewed literature via PubMed and DrugBank. AlphaFold values are estimated from pLDDT confidence scores, not measured binding assays. All Proteus values are computational predictions; no wet-lab validation has been performed. Results should be interpreted as design hypotheses, not confirmed affinities.

Features

Everything you need for protein design

From MCMC generation to 3D visualization — a complete autonomous pipeline.

Generation

MCMC Engine

Parallel tempering with configurable temperatures. Metropolis-Hastings acceptance. BLOSUM62 and ESM-2 guided proposals for biologically plausible mutations.

Scoring

Multi-Objective Energy

Binding affinity, folding stability, solubility, charge, and hydrophobicity — weighted and optimized simultaneously. Customizable per target.

Iteration

Autonomous Improvement

Each round uses the best candidate as the next seed. More MCMC steps and chains per round for progressively finer convergence.

Visualization

3D Structure Viewer

Interactive viewer with mutation highlighting. Load any PDB file and see designed mutations displayed on the protein backbone in real time.

Audit

Full Traceability

Every mutation proposed, every acceptance decision, every energy delta recorded. Downloadable JSON with complete run history and parameters.

Clinical Translation

Lab-Ready Designs

Every output includes ΔG < −6.5 kcal/mol scoring, synthesis feasibility assessment, delivery constraints (CPP/NLS), and estimated synthesis cost and timeline.

Clinical Translation

Ready for the bench, day one

Every Proteus design ships with the information a wet-lab team needs to synthesize, validate, and deliver.

Binding

Lab-Worthy Affinity

Physics-based ΔG calculated from VdW packing, electrostatics, solvation, and H-bonds. Target: ΔG < −6.5 kcal/mol — benchmarked against Erlotinib, Pembrolizumab, and Sotorasib.

Synthesis

SPPS Feasibility Score

Automated 0–100 synthesis feasibility score covering problematic sequences (Pro-rich, Cys pairs, aromatic clusters), solubility, and estimated cost and timeline.

Delivery

Delivery Constraints

CPP conjugation requirements, nuclear localisation signal needs, disulfide cyclisation options, and serum half-life predictions — per design, per target.

Selectivity

Off-Target Risk

On-target vs off-target selectivity ratio with toxicity flag. ΔΔG selectivity decomposition identifies candidates with >5× selectivity for safe lead progression.
