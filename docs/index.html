Features

Everything you need for protein design

From MCMC generation to 3D visualization — a complete autonomous pipeline.

Generation

MCMC Engine

Parallel tempering with configurable temperatures. Metropolis-Hastings acceptance. BLOSUM62-guided proposals with ESM-2 embeddings.

Scoring

Multi-Objective Energy

Binding affinity, folding stability, solubility, charge, hydrophobicity — weighted and optimized simultaneously.

Iteration

Autonomous Improvement

Each round uses the best candidate as the next seed. Steps and chains increase per round for finer convergence.

Visualization

3D Structure Viewer

Interactive 3Dmol.js viewer. Load any PDB, highlight designed mutations in red on a white cartoon backbone.

Audit

Full Traceability

Every mutation, acceptance, energy delta recorded. Downloadable JSON with complete run history and parameters.

Compliance

Research Use Only

Audit logging for all actions. Dual-use screening. Data retention policies. Not a medical device.

Who It's For

Designed for researchers, by researchers

Proteus bridges computational and wet-lab scientists in the protein design workflow.

🧬

Computational Biologists

Run in-silico protein design experiments without writing MCMC code. Configure parameters, launch runs, analyze results — all through the chat interface.

🔬

Wet-Lab Researchers

Upload a target PDB, describe the clinical context, and receive ranked candidates with binding scores and 3D structures ready for validation.

💊

Drug Discovery Teams

Accelerate early-stage discovery. Generate diverse candidate libraries in minutes. Iterate on designs with autonomous improvement rounds.

🎓

Students & Educators

Learn protein design fundamentals through an explainable pipeline. Every MCMC step, energy calculation, and mutation decision is surfaced.

Impact

Reducing protein design from months to minutes

Autonomous MCMC replaces manual iterative design cycles.

Traditional workflow: Select target → literature review → design candidate → order synthesis → wait weeks → test → repeat.
Proteus workflow: Enter patient data → describe target → autonomous MCMC → ranked candidates with 3D structures → iterate in minutes.

The goal is not to replace wet-lab validation — it is to reduce the design cycle so researchers can explore more candidate space before committing to synthesis.
